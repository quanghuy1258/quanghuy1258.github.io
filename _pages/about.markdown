My name is Van-Quang-Huy NGUYEN, a teaching assistant at Faculty of Information Technology, University of Science, Viet Nam National University - Ho Chi Minh City.
In 2019, I received my BSc degree in Computer Science from Honors Program at the university I am currently working.
After the graduation, along with working at the university as a teaching assistant, I continued to study for a master's degree.
I just defended my master's thesis successfully at the end of last year and I am awaiting my master's degree.
Meanwhile, I will come back to my English improvement to fulfill foreign language requirements for PhD students later.
Therefore, this blog is a great opportunity for me not to only develop my writing skill in English, especially academic writing, but also review my knowledge in cryptology and other fields.

The name of this blog, Marginal Ideas, derives from a habit I used to have when I was a high school student.
At that time, I often noted solutions on mathematical problem sheets directly.
Every space of those sheets was utilized, even their margins.
That sounds like the fun story about Pierre de Fermat and his last theorem.
Thus, Marginal Ideas will become a collection of my ideas for all problems, a big new "mathematical problem sheet".

Please feel free to read posts.

I am very glad to answer and get acquaintance with you.
